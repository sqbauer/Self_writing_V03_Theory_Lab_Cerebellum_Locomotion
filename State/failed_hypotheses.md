# Failed Hypotheses Registry

This file records hypotheses that were discarded, weakened, or contradicted during theory development.

Keeping this record prevents the system from repeatedly rediscovering and reusing weak explanations.

---

## FH1 — Cerebellum primarily controls locomotor rhythm generation

Status: rejected

Reason:
Evidence strongly suggests that rhythm generation is primarily implemented by spinal central pattern generators rather than cerebellar circuits.

Implication:
The cerebellum likely modulates locomotor parameters rather than generating the locomotor rhythm itself.

---

## FH2 — Cerebellar function during locomotion is purely reactive

Status: weakened

Reason:
Experimental evidence shows cerebellar involvement in predictive motor control and adaptation, suggesting a role in feedforward adjustment rather than purely reactive correction.

Implication:
The cerebellum likely participates in predictive control of locomotion.
