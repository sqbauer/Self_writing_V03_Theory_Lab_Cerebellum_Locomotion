# Nguyen & Person 2025 — Cerebellar Control of Locomotor Adaptation

## Citation
Nguyen, H. and Person, A. (2025)

## Core Question
How does the cerebellum contribute to adaptive locomotor control?

## Key Findings
- Cerebellar activity correlates with locomotor adaptation.
- Purkinje cells encode signals related to locomotor error.
- Perturbations of cerebellar circuits impair adaptive locomotor behavior.

## Mechanistic Implications
These findings support the idea that cerebellar circuits participate in predictive control during locomotion rather than only reactive correction.

## Evidence Relevant to Current Theory
Supports:
- Predictive locomotor control hypothesis
- Error-driven learning mechanisms
- Purkinje cell involvement in adaptive locomotion

## Open Questions
- What variables are actually predicted?
- How is locomotor context encoded in cerebellar inputs?
- What is the role of microzones during locomotor adaptation?
